Vegan Blueberry Muffins

These Vegan Blueberry Muffins are delicious! Moist, light, fluffy, and packed full of sweet, juicy blueberries. And the best is that besides vegan, they are gluten-free and refined sugar free. Perfect for breakfast or as a snack! The recipe is from Rhian's Recipes
She has various recipes but we only have made her blueberry muffin recipe.

The recipe is very easy to make, all you need is: a bowl, a scale(optional) measuring cups, a spatula/wooden spoon, and a muffin baking tray. That's it!

Ingredients

  • 60 grams (1/4 cup) coconut oil
  • 200 ml unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 8 tablespoons maple syrup
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt
  • 150 grams (1 1/4 cup) ground almonds
  • 150 grams (1 1/4 cup) gluten-free flour blend
  • 2 heaped teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 100 grams (1 cup) fresh blueberries

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 180 degrees Celcius (350 Fahrenheit)
  2. Place the coconut oil in a large bowl and melt over a saucepan of boiling water or in the microwave (skip this step if using any other oil)
  3. Once melted, add the milk to the same bowl along with the lemon juice, maple syrup, vanilla, salt and ground almonds
  4. Sift in the flour, baking powder and baking soda
  5. Mix well, adding a tiny splash more milk if it’s looking too dry
  6. Add the fresh blueberries and fold in gently, to make sure you don’t crush them
  7. Transfer the mixture between muffin cases in a muffin tin
  8. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes until risen and an inserted skewer comes out clean

Substitutes you can make

  • Use any type of oil
  • Use any type of plant-based milk
  • Use any type of liquid sweetener
  • You can replace the ground almonds (almond meal/almond flour) with ground walnuts.
  • For a nut-free version: replace the ground almonds (almond meal/almond flour) with ground sunflower seeds or use 30g (¼ cup) coconut flour instead of 150g (1 ¼ cup) ground almonds.
  • You can use plain flour, wholemeal (whole wheat) flour or spelt flour instead of the gluten-free flour if you’re not gluten-free.
  • For an oil-free/no oil version: replace the oil with a few tablespoons of applesauce!

Enjoy!

About me

Hi I'm Sabrina Roestenburg, an aspiring front-end developer and homeshool mom. Welcome to Gluten-free tested and approved.

About a year ago, I learned that one of my daughters had to start a dairy and gluten-free diet. (and to make it even easier coconut, palm oil and cacao are a no-no too!) As a family that eats lots of bread, pasta and pancakes it was quite an adjustment. For pasta I quickly found a decent store bought substitute made out of brown rice, but for bread, pancakes, cupcakes,cookies etc, I had come up with my own game plan. There are thousands of recipes available online but I soon found out that not all of them are as good as they say they are. After much trial and error we have gathered a notebook full of recipes that we like, and like to share.
